## Discovery

### Design Challenges

Today's digital archive inventories tend to suffer from bulky directories, unrefined navigation, dense "wall of text" pages, and thematic voids. For archive visitors, viewing source material can become burdensome once one has already experienced the drawn-out, often fragmented journey of search and discovery.

Beyond the ability to comfortably browse, target, and sort through large collections of archived artifacts, we want to offer visitors an enjoyable unique journey through historically important primary source material.

### Design Goals

- Robust navigation architecture
- Formal, visually cohesive taxonomy structures
- Finely proportioned content layouts
- Clear content divarication
- Embody artifact authenticity

## Storytelling

### Design Challenges

When reading on the screen, eyes can easily be distracted and attention can quickly be misdirected if the text has been crudely rendered, the shape of the screen (or page) neglected, or elements carry gauche decoration. The site will need to accommodate long-form content that is both informational and thoughtful; rich in imagery and text.

We want readers to enjoy their experience with emboldened confidence and control; to move rhythmically through text, documents, and illustrations with ease, and without the threat of displacement.

### Design Goals

- Practical, stylistically sensitive typography
- Informed, exceptionally composed imagery
- Immersive page headers and openings
- Contextual, progressive navigation
- Thematic, character-driven UI and literary accessories

## Typography

### Design Challenges

The right font for both task and audience is often determined with care and precision. Practically speaking, fonts used ought to be decisive and work at the behest of the user in varying contexts; and any hand in styling should be strategic and modest. Furthermore, we want the font to breathe the spirit of the NAACP LDF; permitting the authors to weave consistent paths of varying tones with both compassion and command.

Ultimately, the typography will help form a harmonious relationship between the artifacts on-screen and the actual people, politics, and historical events that have shaped our history.

### Design Goals

- Clarity and legibility at varying sizes and weights
- Application to a wide range of data points and complex structures
- Optical balance and tonal continuity across features
- Compatibility and harmony between font pairings
- Historical inspiration
